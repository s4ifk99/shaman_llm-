#  Making a plan to pay your debts

###  If a creditor won’t freeze interest on your debt

Your creditors don’t have to freeze the interest on your debts. They can refuse to do so, or lower the amount of interest instead of stopping it completely.

If this happens [use the ‘freeze interest’ sample letter on the National Debtline website](https://nationaldebtline.org/get-information/sample-letters/freeze-interest/) to write to them again.

The more interest you pay the longer it will take to pay your debts off. If your creditor won’t freeze interest on your debts [contact your nearest Citizens Advice](/about-us/contact-us/contact-us/contact-us/). An adviser can negotiate with your creditors or look at other ways of paying your debts.

## Stay in touch with your creditors

You’re responsible for making the agreed repayments and for keeping your creditors up to date about your circumstances.

You should contact your creditors every few months to let them know your circumstances haven’t changed. This will show them you’re serious about dealing with your debt.

Your creditors can still take court action against you after you've agreed a repayment plan. [Find out what to do if a creditor takes you to court for debt](/debt-and-money/action-your-creditor-can-take/if-youre-being-taken-to-court-for-debt/).

Even if you’ve agreed repayments with your creditors, there might be other ways you could deal with your debts. [Check your options for getting out of debt](/debt-and-money/help-with-debt/dealing-with-your-debts/check-your-debt-options/).
