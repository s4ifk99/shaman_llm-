#  Problems with work if you have a Health and Care Worker visa

####  If you're a health worker

The minimum rate depends on when you were first sponsored for your job. You can find out when you were sponsored on your certificate.

If you were first sponsored on or after 9 April 2025, your minimum salary is £25,000 a year. For example, if you work 37.5 hours each week, you should be paid at least £12.82 each hour.

If you were first sponsored before 9 April 2025, you only need to check that your employer is paying you the National Minimum Wage. The old minimum rates they had to pay are less than the current National Minimum Wage.

Your job also has a ‘going rate’ - this is an amount set by the NHS. The going rate will depend on the NHS pay bands and how many years’ experience you have.

You should be paid at least 80% of the going rate for your job. If 80% of the going rate is less than the minimum rate, your employer must pay you at least the minimum rate.

Example

Ade has got a job as a nursing support worker on band 4 of the pay scale.

The going rate for the job is £26,530 a year for 37.5 hours a week - this is the going rate.

Ade should get at least 80% of the going rate, unless it’s less than the minimum rate of £25,000

80% of £26,530 is £21,224. This is less than the minimum rate.

Ade must be paid at least the minimum rate of £25,000

You can [check the going rate for your job on the NHS Employers website](https://www.nhsemployers.org/articles/pay-scales-202223).

If your employer isn’t paying you the correct amount, you can ask them to pay the money they owe you.

You can [check what to do if you're having problems getting paid and how to complain](/work/pay/problems-getting-paid/).

### Check you’re getting the National Minimum Wage

Your employer shouldn’t pay you less than the National Minimum Wage. If you’re 23 or over, they shouldn’t pay you less than the National Living Wage.

They might be paying you less if they’ve deducted money from your wages - for example for travel, training or accommodation.

You can [check the National Minimum Wage and National Living Wage and what to do if you’re being paid less](/work/pay/getting-paid-less-than-minimum-wage-or-living-wage/).

## If your employer deducted money from your wages or charged you fees

You might have been charged fees or had money deducted by your employer. If you’re not sure what the fees or deduction is for, you can check your payslip or ask your employer.

Your employer can only deduct money for something you’ve agreed to pay - you need to have agreed to it before they make the deduction. You can check what you’ve agreed to pay for by checking your employment contract or written agreement.

Your employer has to pay you the National Minimum Wage - this means they usually can’t deduct money if it would mean they’re paying you less.

If your contract and Health and Care Worker Visa started on or after 9 April 2025, your employer has to ensure your salary is at least £25,000.

If your employer deducts money for loans, investments or immigration charges, your salary must still be at least £25,000 after the deductions. They can deduct money for National Insurance, Income Tax and your pension contributions, even if it takes your salary below £25,000

You can [check what to do if you’re having problems getting paid and how to complain](/work/pay/problems-getting-paid/).

### If your employer has asked you to pay sponsor fees

You shouldn’t have to pay any of the following sponsor fees:

  * the Sponsor Licence fee

  * the Certificate of Sponsorship fee

  * the Immigration Skills Charge

Your employer also can't charge you for their time and effort paying these fees. This includes if they paid a third party to do this. They might call this an 'administration charge'.

If your employer is telling you to pay sponsor fees, you can [talk to an adviser](/about-us/contact-us/contact-us/contact-us/).

## If a recruitment agency charged you fees

If you were recruited through an agency in the UK, there are rules they have to follow.

If you were recruited by an agency outside the UK, they have to follow the rules in the country where they’re based.

### If you were recruited by an agency in the UK

The agency isn’t allowed to charge you a fee for finding you a job.

The agency is allowed to charge you fees for other things. For example they can charge fees for:

  * helping you write your CV

  * helping you move to the UK

If you’ll be charged a fee for the agency’s work, they must tell you about the fee and you must agree to it before they start the work.

If a recruitment agency has charged you fees and you think they shouldn’t have, you can [talk to an adviser](/about-us/contact-us/contact-us/contact-us/).

## Problems with how many hours you work

If you’re not sure how many hours you’re supposed to work, you should check your contract. It should tell you:

  * the number of hours your employer has to give you

  * the usual times you’ll be working each week

  * how much overtime you can do

You can [find out how to check your employment contract](/work/basic-rights-and-contracts/contracts-of-employment/).

###  If your employer isn’t giving you enough hours

You should talk to your employer to try and sort out the problem.

You might still be entitled to be paid for the hours you were supposed to work, even if there was no work.

Your employer might be giving you fewer hours because they don’t have enough work for you. You can [check what to do if your employer doesn’t have enough work for you](/work/redundancy/check-your-rights-if-youre-made-redundant/if-youre-laid-off/layoffs-and-short-time-working/).

If your employer’s situation has changed since they applied for their licence and they have less work for you, they must tell the Home Office within 20 days. If the Home Office decide to cancel your visa, they’ll write to you to tell you your Health and Care Worker visa has ended.

###  If you think you’re working too many hours

Your employer can’t make you work more than 48 hours a week on average.

If you travel as part of your job, for example if you care for people in their homes, you should count time spent travelling as working time.

You can [check if you’re working more than the 48-hour limit](/work/working-hours-and-rest-breaks/check-how-many-hours-youre-working/).

If you’re working less than the 48-hour limit, you might still feel you're working too many hours if you:

  * have mental or physical health problems because of overwork

  * work more overtime than it says in your contract or more than you think is fair

  * are asked to do more than it's realistic to achieve in your normal working hours

You can [check what to do if you’re working too many hours](/work/working-hours-and-rest-breaks/if-youre-working-too-many-hours/).

## If you’re having other problems at work

You can get help - for example you can get help with:

  * getting paid if you're too sick to work - [check if you can get sick pay](/work/sick-leave-and-sick-pay/check-if-you-can-get-sick-pay/)

  * getting paid when you're on holiday - [check if you can get paid holiday](/work/holidays-and-holiday-pay/check-if-youre-entitled-to-paid-holidays/)

You can also get help if you’ve experienced discrimination - you can [check if your problem at work is discrimination](/work/discrimination-at-work/discrimination-at-work/checking-if-its-discrimination/check-if-your-problem-at-work-is-discrimination/).

If you need help with a different problem, you can [get help with problems at work](/work/).

## If you want to find a new employer to sponsor you

Before you leave your job, you should check your employment contract to find out your responsibilities and rights. For example, you should check if your employer can charge you fees.

[Talk to an adviser](/about-us/contact-us/contact-us/contact-us/) if you want to leave your job and you’re not sure about your rights.

You’ll need to find a new employer to sponsor you.

### Check an employer is a licensed sponsor holder

Your new employer must be a licensed sponsor holder so they can give you a new Certificate of Sponsorship. You can [find the list of licensed sponsor holders on the Autonomy website](https://autonomy.work/care-visa-sponsor-database/).

If an employer isn’t on the list, it’s best to find a different employer.

### Check an employer is registered with the Care Quality Commission

Your new employer must be registered with the Care Quality Commission.

You can [check if your employer is registered on the CQC website](https://www.cqc.org.uk/care-services).

### If you’re using a recruitment agency

You can [check if the agency is on the list of ethical recruiters on GOV.UK](https://www.nhsemployers.org/articles/ethical-recruiters-list). If they’re on the list it means they’ve said they’ll follow the government’s rules about recruitment.

### Updating your Health and Care Worker visa

When you’ve found a new employer, you’ll need to apply to update your visa.

If your partner or children have visas that depend on your visa, you’ll need to update their visas as well.

[Check how to update your visa on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/health-care-worker-visa/update-your-visa-if-you-change-job-or-employer).

## If the Home Office revoke your employer’s sponsorship licence

If the Home Office sends you a letter saying they’ve ‘revoked' your employer’s licence, this means they’ve cancelled it.

They'll usually cancel your visa 60 days after they cancelled your employer's licence.

You should email your nearest 'regional partnership' straight away. They can help you find a new employer who can sponsor you.

You can find details of the regional partnership in the letter from the Home Office. You can also [find your regional partnership’s email address on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/support-offer-to-international-asc-workers-whose-employers-sponsor-licence-has-been-revoked/support-offer-to-international-asc-workers-whose-employers-sponsor-licence-has-been-revoked).

Make sure your email includes:

  * your name

  * your current address

  * your contact details, for example your email address and phone number

  * the name of the employer who’s sponsoring your visa

### If you find a new employer

You should apply to switch to a Skilled Worker visa, using your new employer’s certificate of sponsorship. Make sure you apply before your current visa is cancelled. You’ll have to pay a fee - [check the fees for Skilled Worker visas on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/how-much-it-costs).

You can [apply to switch to a Skilled Worker visa on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa/switch-to-this-visa).

You might be able to use the ‘priority service’ to get a decision on your visa application within 5 working days. There’s usually a fee for this, but the regional partnership might be able to get the priority service for free.

You can [find out about the ‘priority service’ on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/faster-decision-visa-settlement).
