#  You've been told your flat is fire safe but disagree

The first thing you should do is look at your fire safety assessment - you can ask your landlord or the council for a copy, depending who arranged the assessment. If you haven’t arranged for an assessment yet you should ask for one.

Look at the findings from the assessment - if you don’t understand parts of the assessment, or you still disagree, explain this to your landlord.

## Collect evidence

Get together any evidence you have that your home is unsafe - for example you should:

  * get a copy of your fire safety assessment

  * get copies of any letters or conversations you’ve had with your landlord about the problem

  * write down the reasons you disagree with the assessment

  * ask for a report from the fire service

## Find out how to make a complaint

You can go to court instead of complaining to your landlord. [Check if you should take your landlord to court](/housing/repairs-and-housing/repairs-and-housing-conditions/getting-your-landlord-to-do-repairs/going-to-court-if-your-landlord-wont-do-repairs/).

Who you need to contact depends whether you’re renting from:

  * the council

  * a private landlord

  * a housing association

Check your tenancy agreement to find out how to make a complaint. If you're a council tenant, you can usually [find the complaints process on their website](https://www.gov.uk/find-local-council).

You should put your complaint in writing - clearly write down why you disagree with the assessment, and what you want to happen next. For example whether you want another assessment, or for them to repair the fire risks in the building.

You should include details of any repairs that your landlord should have done - [check which repairs need to be made by a landlord](/housing/repairs-and-housing/repairs-and-housing-conditions/whos-responsible-for-repairs/check-if-your-landlord-has-to-do-repairs/). Make a copy of your complaint in case you need it later.

If you’re not sure who to complain to, or you need help writing it down - [talk to an adviser](/about-us/contact-us/). An adviser can help you make a formal complaint.

You can also raise the issue with your local MP - they might be able to take your concerns directly to the council. [Find your local MP on the UK Parliament website](https://members.parliament.uk/members/commons).

### Complaining if you rent from a private landlord

If you're not happy with your landlord's response, [speak to your local council on GOV.UK](https://members.parliament.uk/members/commons). Ask them to do an inspection - they’ll investigate if they think there is a hazard in the home you rent. They’ll decide whether or not your landlord has to make changes based on this inspection.

If your council doesn’t think there are any hazards that need addressing, and you disagree, you can [complain about your local council to the Local Government Ombudsman](http://www.lgo.org.uk/).

Your landlord can’t evict you just because you complain. They can only evict you for specific reasons - for example if you have rent arrears or they decide to sell the property.

Your landlord has to give you a document called a ‘section 8 notice’ to evict you. They have to use the right process and explain on the notice why they want you to leave. You can [check if the reason on your section 8 notice is valid](/housing/eviction/getting-evicted/renting-privately/check-your-section-8-notice/).

If your landlord gives you a section 21 notice on or after 1 May 2026, it’s not valid. If they gave you the section 21 notice before then, you should [check if your section 21 notice is still valid](/housing/eviction/getting-evicted/renting-privately/if-you-get-a-section-21-notice-from-a-private-landlord/).

### Complaining if you rent from a housing association

If you're not happy with your landlord's response, [speak to your local council on GOV.UK](https://members.parliament.uk/members/commons). Ask them to do an inspection - they’ll investigate if they think there is a hazard in the home you rent. They’ll decide whether or not your landlord has to make changes based on this inspection.

If your council doesn’t think there are any hazards that need addressing, and you disagree, you can [complain about your local council to the Local Government Ombudsman](http://www.lgo.org.uk/).

### Complaining if you rent from the council

[Ask your local council to arrange another inspection](/housing/fire-safety/make-sure-your-flat-is-fire-safe/). They’ll then have to decide whether or not to make changes if the inspection finds any hazards.

If they won’t do another inspection or you’re unhappy with the outcome, you can complain to [the Local Government Ombudsman](http://www.lgo.org.uk/).

## If your complaint isn't addressed

You might be able to take your complaint further if you’re still not happy, but you’ll need to get specialist legal advice.

Contact [your nearest Citizens Advice](/about-us/contact-us/contact-us/contact-us/) \- an adviser can help you find a local solicitor or another service offering free legal advice.
