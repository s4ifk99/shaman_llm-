#  Check if your energy meter isn't working properly

###  How gas meters are tested

Your supplier will take your meter away to be tested by an independent examiner.

Take a reading before the meter is tested. [Check how to read your gas meter](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/your-energy-meter/how-to-read-your-energy-meter/).

You can take a photo of the meter reading and serial number - this can help to avoid any dispute if you get a bill. If you can’t find the serial number, ask your supplier what it is.

The test is free. However, if the test shows that the meter is accurate, your supplier will charge you for removing the meter and installing a replacement. The cost will vary between suppliers but might be around £200.

[Check what you can expect if you ask for a gas meter test on GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/gas-meter-accuracy-and-disputes).

### Book an appointment with your supplier to test your meter

Your supplier should offer you an appointment as soon as they can. This depends on how urgent your appointment is and how busy they are.

Appointments are usually within normal working hours. Working hours usually means between 9am and 5pm. You usually have to choose a time slot between 2 and 4 hours.

If they offer an appointment that doesn't work for you, ask for a different date and time. Your supplier should say yes if they can.

Your supplier must pay you compensation if they:

  * miss the appointment

  * change the appointment without your agreement and with less than 1 working day’s notice

  * send someone without the right qualifications or tools to do the work

They’ll pay you £40 compensation within 10 working days. If they haven’t paid within 10 working days, they’ll pay you another £40.

## After your meter is tested

The independent examiner will send you a certificate telling you if the meter is accurate or not.

### If your meter is inaccurate

Your supplier should replace it. They should also work out how much you should have been charged while your meter was faulty.

If you’ve been charged too much your supplier should refund you any money they owe you.

If you’ve been charged too little, they might ask for extra payment. If you disagree with the amount you’ve been charged, you should complain to your supplier.

Your supplier might only be able to charge you for the energy you used in the last 12 months. If your meter has been inaccurate for over 12 months, you should check if any charges go back more than a year. [Check what to do if you haven’t received an accurate energy bill in a while](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/problems-with-your-energy-bill/you-havent-received-a-gas-or-electricity-bill-in-a-while/).

You might be able to claim compensation for inaccurate billing - ask your supplier.

### If your meter is accurate

Your supplier might charge you for removing your meter and installing a replacement. The cost will vary between suppliers, but might be around £200.

## If your meter can’t be tested

Your supplier will not be able to tell if your readings are accurate. This means they’ll have to estimate your energy use. They might use readings from other similar properties to calculate their estimate. If you don’t agree with their estimate, you should complain to your supplier.

## If your supplier has gone bust

You should contact your new supplier to report any problems. You can get their contact details from their website.

It might take a while for your information to be moved from your old supplier to your new supplier. This means your new supplier might not be able to help with your problem straight away.

## If you’re not happy

If you feel that your supplier has not followed the correct procedure, or you’re unhappy with the result, you should [make a complaint to your supplier](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/complain-about-an-energy-company/complain-to-your-energy-supplier/).

## Further help

[Contact the Citizens Advice consumer service](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/help/if-you-need-more-help-about-a-consumer-issue-energy/) if you need more help - a trained adviser can give you advice over the phone or online chat.
