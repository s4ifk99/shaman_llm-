#  Problems with your business energy supplier or broker

####  If your broker is registered with the Utilities Intermediaries Association (UIA)

You can take your complaint to the UIA if you’ve followed your broker’s complaints process and either:

  * it’s been 15 working days since you complained

  * you’re not satisfied with your broker’s response

[Check how to complain about your broker on the UIA website](https://uia.org.uk/uia-complaint-form/).

####  If your broker is registered with the DRO

You can complain to the DRO if it’s been 8 weeks since you used your broker's complaints process and either:

  * your broker hasn’t replied to your complaint

  * you and your broker haven’t resolved the problem

If you get a ‘deadlock letter’ from your broker you can complain to the DRO immediately - you don’t need to wait 8 weeks. A deadlock letter is a final reply to say you can’t agree on how to fix the problem.

If you get a deadlock letter or an offer of settlement you have 1 year to complain to the DRO. This starts from when you get the letter.

If you don’t have a deadlock letter or an offer of settlement you might be able to complain to the DRO after 1 year. It’ll depend on your individual case.

[Check how to complain about your broker on the DRO website](https://www.disputeresolutionombudsman.org/dispute).
