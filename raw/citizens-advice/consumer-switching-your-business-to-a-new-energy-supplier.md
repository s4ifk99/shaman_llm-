#  Switching your business to a new energy supplier

###  If you’re not a microbusiness

Your supplier should tell you how much money your broker is getting for setting up the contract. They need to tell you this in a way that’s easy for you to understand.

Your supplier doesn't have to tell you the total amount, but they must tell you how much money your broker is getting from:

  * each unit of energy your business uses

  * your monthly or daily standing charge

If your broker has misled you, you can complain. For example, your broker might have misled you about the terms of your contract or your tariff being the cheapest.

If you complain, you might be able to cancel your contract or get your money back.

## Contacting your supplier or broker about a problem

If you have a problem with your energy supplier or broker, contact them.

If they can't help you or you aren't happy with their response, you can complain. You'll need to complain using their complaints process.

[Check how to contact your business energy supplier or broker about a problem](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/your-small-businesss-energy-supply/problems-with-your-business-energy-supplier-or-broker/).

## If you need more help

[Contact the Citizens Advice consumer service](/consumer/energy/energy-supply/help/if-you-need-more-help-about-a-consumer-issue-energy/) if you need more help - a trained adviser can give you advice over the phone or online chat.

The advisers can give you unbiased advice about business energy contracts and your rights.
