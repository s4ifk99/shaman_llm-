If you do not live in the Channel Islands, Isle of Man or a British Overseas Territory, you [can apply online](https://visas-immigration.service.gov.uk/product/nationality-services).

You can [get help](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/assisted-digital-service-uk-visas-and-immigration) to make your online application if you are in the UK and you do not have the appropriate access, skills or confidence to complete an online immigration application form.

Use this form if you live in the Channel Islands, Isle of Man or a British Overseas Territory and have lost or damaged a citizenship certificate that was issued after October 1986 and want a new one.

You cannot replace a certificate that was issued before October 1986.

You can also use this paper form if you live elsewhere and want to apply by post. You will need to submit your documents with your application.

You should read the nationality forms guide [to help you fill in the form.](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/nationality-forms-guide-channel-islands-isle-of-man-and-british-overseas-territories)

Send the form and [payment slip (including the fee)](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fees-for-citizenship-applications) and your documents to:

  * the Lieutenant Governor if you are in the Channel Islands or Isle of Man
  * the Governor of the territory if you are in a British Overseas Territory
  * UK Visas and Immigration at the following postal address if you live elsewhere:

Department 102  
UKVI  
The Capital  
New Hall Place  
Liverpool  
L3 9PP
