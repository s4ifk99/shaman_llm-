Sykes Holiday Cottages is one of the largest short-term home rental agencies in Europe. Sykes is owned by Private Equity firm Vitruvian Partners who bought Sykes Cottages in 2019 for £375 million pounds. During Covid, many millions of customers had holiday rentals cancelled and were left chasing refunds from Sykes Cottages and other online travel agents like BudgetAir, eDreams and Opodo. In April 2020, the [BBC wrote an article](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-52394958) exposing some of the cancelled booking refund problems experienced by Sykes customers.

The UK regulator the CMA is even [conducting an investigation](https://www.gov.uk/cma-cases/covid-19-cancellations-airlines) into whether airlines breached consumer rights laws during Coronavirus. But unlike airlines, travel agents aren't regulated.

### How to get a refund on a cancelled Sykes flight?

If you're one of the many unlucky Sykes Cottages customers waiting on a cancelled flight refund due to Coronavirus travel bans, you have a few options. Some options require more time than others, and with varying chances of working.

### What are all the flight refund options available during Covid-19?

#### Option 1: Contact Sykes Cottages

You can call, email or use social media to get in touch directly with Sykes. Unfortunately, this is what everyone else is doing (2 million others) so you'll have to join the queue.

#### Option 2: Use a complaints website

These sites help you send a polite letter of complaint, based on EU law. Unfortunately this can be like screaming at a brick wall (or banging your head against it), since a lot of the time the airline has already reimbursed the travel agent so they claim it's not their problem anymore.

Even worse, if you do win your case through a complaints site, they'll take 25% of your refund, leaving you with no holiday and no money to book one in the future.
