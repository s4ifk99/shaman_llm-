Navigating the world of solicitor fees and costs can be surprisingly challenging. The fees involved can vary greatly, with many different fee types such as fixed fees, no win no fee, and hourly rates. Let's take a look at how much solicitors charge on average and the key factors that influence legal fees, including the legal area, complexity of the case, the solicitor's experience, and their location. By shedding light on these factors, you'll gain a better understanding of solicitor costs and be better equipped to make an informed decision when hiring a solicitor.

### How much does a solicitor cost on average?

#### Legal area

The type of legal work you need can significantly impact the [fees solicitors charge](https://lawhive.co.uk/knowledge-hub/find-a-solicitor/solicitor-pricing-in-2023-comparing-hourly-rates-to-fixed-and-no-win-no-fee/). Different legal areas require varying levels of expertise, specialisation, and experience, all of which contribute to the overall cost. To help you better understand how the legal area affects solicitor fees, we’ve created a table below.

---|---|---

Landlord & Tenant| Moderate| landlord-tenant disputes, evictions

#### Complexity

The complexity of your legal matter will also influence the cost. Complex cases typically require more time, resources, and expertise, which translates into higher fees. Factors that can make legal work more complex include:

Multiple parties involved – When more parties are involved in a case, the number of documents, legal arguments, and negotiation processes often increase, making the case more challenging to manage. This complexity can lead to a higher workload for your solicitor, ultimately resulting in increased fees.

Intricate legal issues – Some cases involve complicated legal issues that require a deeper understanding of the law, extensive research, and the ability to develop creative legal strategies. These types of cases often demand a higher level of expertise from your solicitor, which can increase the cost of their services.

Large amounts of documentation - Cases that involve substantial documentation, such as contracts, correspondence, or financial records, can be time-consuming and labour-intensive for your solicitor to review and analyse. The more time your solicitor spends on these tasks, the higher the fees are likely to be.

Need for specialist input – In some cases, it may be necessary to consult with expert witnesses or other specialists to support your case or provide essential information. These experts can be expensive to retain, and their fees are often passed on to the client, contributing to the overall cost of your legal representation.

#### The solicitor's location

The location of your solicitor can have a significant impact on the fees they charge. This can create real challenges in accessing affordable legal services.

Solicitors who operate in big cities tend to charge more. Higher living expenses, such as rent, utilities, and transportation, can necessitate higher fees to cover these costs and maintain a sustainable business. It can go the other way too though. It’s also very common for solicitors based in remote or rural areas where access to legal services may be limited to charge more. With few solicitors available to handle cases, the scarcity can result in higher fees as the few solicitors in these areas have less competition.

#### The solicitor's experience

A solicitor's level of experience can significantly impact the fees they charge. More experienced solicitors often command higher fees due to their expertise, proven track record, and ability to handle complex cases efficiently. Here's a closer look at how experience factors into solicitor fees:

Years of practice – Solicitors with more years of practice under their belt are often more knowledgeable about the intricacies of the law and have developed a broader set of skills. This expertise enables them to provide better legal advice and representation, which is often reflected in their fees.

Track record – Solicitors who have a history of success in their field or a high rate of positive case outcomes may charge more for their services. Clients are often willing to pay a premium for a solicitor with a proven track record, as it increases their chances of achieving a favourable outcome in their case.

### How much do solicitors charge per hour

Some solicitors will charge you on an hourly basis. This means that the cost of their services is determined by the amount of time they spend working on your case. Understanding the average hourly rates for solicitors can help you gauge what to expect when evaluating potential legal services.

Level of Experience| Expected Cost
---|---
Newly qualified or junior| £100 - £200
Mid-level or experienced| £200 - £300
Senior or highly-specialised| £350 or more

We aren’t big fans of hourly fees at Lawhive. They can be opaque, unclear and often lead to people over paying for their legal work. We’d recommend that you stick with fixed fees instead if given the option. If you are working with a Lawhive solicitor you’ll be paying a fixed fee rather than an hourly one.
