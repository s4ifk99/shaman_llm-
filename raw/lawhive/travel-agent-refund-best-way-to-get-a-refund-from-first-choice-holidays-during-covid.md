First Choice Holidays is part of [TUI Group](https://lawhive.co.uk/knowledge-hub/flight-refund/best-way-to-get-a-cancelled-flight-refund-from-tui-during-covid/), the largest travel and tourism company in the world, selling holidays to about 27 million people in 2019. However during Covid-19, First Choice had to cancel 90% of its holiday bookings leaving millions of paying passengers in the lurch.

The UK regulator the CMA is even [conducting an investigation](https://www.gov.uk/cma-cases/covid-19-cancellations-airlines) into whether airlines breached consumer rights laws during Coronavirus. But unlike airlines, travel agents aren't regulated.

### How to get a refund on a cancelled First Choice flight?

If you're one of the many unlucky First Choice Holidays customers waiting on a cancelled flight refund due to Coronavirus travel bans, you have a few options. Some options require more time than others, and with varying chances of working.

### What are all the flight refund options available during Covid-19?

#### Option 1: Contact First Choice Holidays

You can call, email or use social media to get in touch directly with First Choice. Unfortunately, this is what everyone else is doing (2 million others) so you'll have to join the queue.

#### Option 2: Use a complaints website

These sites help you send a polite letter of complaint, based on EU law. Unfortunately this can be like screaming at a brick wall (or banging your head against it), since a lot of the time the airline has already reimbursed the travel agent so they claim it's not their problem anymore.

Even worse, if you do win your case through a complaints site, they'll take 25% of your refund, leaving you with no holiday and no money to book one in the future.
