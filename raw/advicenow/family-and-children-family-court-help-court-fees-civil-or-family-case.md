#  How to apply for help with family court fees

You may be able to get help with court fees or not have to pay a fee at all, if you are on a low income or get certain welfare benefits. This is called being 'exempt' from paying court fees. Here, we explain what help is available and how to apply.

This information applies to England and Wales.

### At a glance

  1. If you get certain benefits like income support, UC (with earnings no more than £6,000) JSA or ESA, you automatically get help with court fees as long as you have no or little savings – so that you don’t need to pay the fee at all.
  2. You need to have a low income to get help with fees. You can either get a reduced fee or pay no fee at all depending on your income.
  3. Savings – you must have little or no savings. If your court fee is £1,420 or less (most court fees are) then you must have less than £4,250 in savings unless you are 66 years or older.
  4. If you have a partner, their income counts too unless they are (or will be) involved in the court application and you don’t agree on the case.
  5. Ideally, you apply for help with fees when you make your application, but you can ask for a refund later, within 3 months, as long as you were entitled to help with fees at the time.

##  Family court fees

Depending on your situation, you might be able to get some help with the fee or you may not have to pay a court fee at all, if you are on a low income or get certain benefits. This is called help with fees (HwF) or getting a 'fee remission'.

### Applying to court

If you are going to court, you usually have to pay a fee if you are the person starting the case. Sometimes you may have to pay other court fees too later on – depending on what happens in the case.

Maybe the case you are involved in was started by someone else? If so there may be times when you want to make an application yourself, for example, to apply for a financial order in a divorce case. This will usually involve you having to pay a court fee.

For information about the different court fees and when and how to pay them, see court information leaflet EX50 [Civil and family court fees](/node/11250 "Civil and family court fees").

Read on to find out how to get help with any court fees you need to pay. You can also watch a helpful video from the court service that gives you an overview of the process.

##  Working out if you can get help with court fees

Open

Close

### Who is exempt from paying court fees?

You will only be able to get help with fees if you are on certain benefits or have a low income and have little in the way of savings.

### Benefits

If you get any of the following benefits you can get help with court fees:

  * Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  * Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  * Income Support
  * Universal Credit (and you earn less than £6,000 a year)
  * Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit)

You must also have less than the maximum amount of money in savings. This is usually up to £4,250, unless you are 66 years old, or older, when the limit goes up to £16,000.

The court checks with DWP to confirm that you are getting one of these benefits.

### Savings

Usually, you need to have less than £4,250 in savings to get help with fees, unless you are 66 years old or older.

### Income

You, and any partner you have, need to be on a low income to get help with court fees. The level on income you can have and still get help depends on if you have a partner or children or both.

You can work out if you are likely to be entitled to help by using the online [Fee Remissions Contribution Calculator (EX 160C)](/node/16562 "Fee remission calculator "). You need to put in your gross income not your take home money.



#### What is 'gross income'?

You need to use figures for your gross income for your application for help with fees. This is the amount you earn each month before you pay tax and national insurance.

##  How to apply for help with court fees

Open

Close

### Apply online or on a paper form

You can do an online application, or you can fill in form EX160 and print it off. Either way you need to go to the [GOV.UK – help with fees page](/node/13322 "Get help paying court and tribunal fees"). If you do the online application you will get a ‘help with fees reference’. You might see this written as 'HWF'.

You need to use the code in the court application you want to make. If you want to do a paper form but don’t have access to a printer you can ask your [nearest court](/node/10158 "Find the right court or tribunal") to send you a paper form.

You need to complete a separate application for each court fee you want reduced or cancelled. This may mean you have to apply more than once during your case.

### Applying for help with fees when your case is urgent

If you need to make an emergency application for a court order you can still ask for help with fees. How you do this depends on whether you decide to do the application online or on paper.

#### 1\. Asking for help with fees for an urgent application – online

When you have done your online Help with fees application you get a code. You need to add this code to your online application for your hearing. Urgent online applications get set by court staff to your local court within 24 hours. The court should process your help with fees application within 5 working days if you are making an urgent application. If you believe your application is so urgent it needs to be dealt with on the day you make it or the day after you can call your local court to explain.

####
2\. Asking for help with fees for an urgent application – on paper

If you do your main application on paper, there is a box to tick to say that it is urgent. Ticking this box will mean that court staff see the application for help with fees needs to be processed quickly. Make sure you put your help with fees paper form with your main application, or if you do the help with fees application online, don’t forget to add the code to the paperwork for your main application. The court should process your help with fees application within 5 working days if you are making an urgent application. If you believe your application is so urgent it needs to be dealt with on the day you make it or the day after you can take the paperwork to your local court yourself.

##  What do you need to apply for help with fees?

Open

Close

### You will need

  * Your National Insurance number, or a Home Office reference number.
  * The court form number – you will find this at the bottom of the form you are filling in to make your application or on the online application.
  * Case number - if you have one.
  * Details of all your income for example: wages, maintenance, benefits, pensions, financial help from other people like family.
  * if you have a partner, your partner’s financial details covering the same period – unless your court or tribunal case is against them (for example an application to get divorced or dissolve your civil partnership).

The court will assess your application and might contact you to ask you to provide evidence of your financial situation. It’s really important to respond quickly if they do this so that your application isn’t held up.

##  FAQs

Open

Close

### I can’t get legal aid, so what’s the point of applying for help to pay my court fee?

The two schemes aren’t linked. There may be no legal aid available in your kind of case or even if there is, you might not be entitled to it. Or maybe your application was refused. Either way, that doesn’t stop you applying for help to pay your court fee.

### I want to apply for help with court fees. Do I need to include my partner’s income?

Yes – you need to include your partner's income unless the application is against them – for example in a dispute about where your children will live.

### What if my income changes month to month?

You can use your income from the last calendar month or an average of the last 3 months if that income is lower and more in line with your usual income.

### Can I get help with fees if I have already paid?

Yes. You can apply for a refund for any court fee paid in the last 3 months, as long as you were entitled to get the help at the time you paid the fee. Use form EX160 and make sure you give the court evidence of your financial situation at the time you paid the fee.

### I applied for help to pay my court fee but was refused. Is there any more I can do?

You can appeal. If you decide to appeal make sure you do so by the date in the letter or email you get from the court refusing your application. You appeal by writing to the court explaining why you are unhappy with their decision and including any evidence that will support your appeal.

### I am not able to get help with fees but I really can't afford to pay. What can I do?

If you believe that you really cannot afford the fee and will face exceptional hardship if you have to pay it, you can explain this to the court. They might ask for more information to help them decide in your personal case not to charge you. For more information about this see the section headed ‘If you are likely to experience exceptional hardship’ in the court leaflet called [How to apply with fees EX160A.](/node/12673 "Apply for help with fees")
