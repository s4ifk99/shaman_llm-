#  WCA mandatory reconsideration letter tool

Free tool to challenge a 'fit for work' decision. Tell the DWP why they got your work capability assessment decision wrong. Designed by benefits advisers to help you get the right ESA or LCWRA decision.

[Quality checked](/get-help/why-you-can-trust-our-content)

This information applies to England and Wales. It will also be helpful for claimants in Northern Ireland.

Last updated: March 2026

Welcome to our tool that makes it easy to challenge an ESA or limited capability for work decision. We know that the DWP makes wrong decisions about assessments very frequently, and we want to help you to get the correct amount of benefit if you're not well enough to work or look for work.



According[ to research](/research-and-policy/reports/using-personalised-legal-support-tools-help-benefits-claimants-challenge "Using personalised legal support tools to help benefits claimants challenge decisions"), using one of our mandatory reconsideration tools more than doubles your chances of getting the decision changed at the mandatory reconsideration stage. Don’t wait for the appeal to make your case.

Don’t worry, it is not too late \- even if 30 days have passed. You should still ask for a mandatory reconsideration if you got the decision less than 13 months ago.

####  Steps to appeal a work capability assessment decision

  1. Ask DWP to look at the 'fit for work' decision again. Use this tool to explain why it was wrong.
  2. If the decision is not changed, ask for[ work capability assessment appeal](/get-help/benefits/universal-credit-uc/appeal-work-capability-assessment-decision "Appeal a work capability assessment decision").
  3. Prepare for appeal and [write tribunal submission](/get-help/benefits/universal-credit-uc/work-capability-assessment-tribunal-submission-tool "Work Capability Assessment Tribunal Submission Tool").
  4. [Tribunal hearing](/get-help/benefits/universal-credit-uc/work-capability-assessment-tribunal-hearings "Work capability assessment tribunal hearings").

Follow our advice in [Appeal a work capability assessment decision](/get-help/benefits/universal-credit-uc/appeal-work-capability-assessment-decision "Appeal a work capability assessment decision").
