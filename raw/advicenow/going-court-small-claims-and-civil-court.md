#  Small claims and the civil court

### How to take someone to court

Step-by-step help to understand how to take somone to court for unpaid debt, a broken contract, consumer problems, or personal injury (including road traffic accidents) - and if that is really a good idea.

The information in this section applies to England and Wales.

Last updated: April 2026

[Quality checked](/get-help/why-you-can-trust-our-content)

![null](/sites/default/files/styles/page_hero_image/public/2024-12/older-man-woman.jpg.webp?itok=7HKUbe8H)

The advice guides on this page explain different steps in the process for taking a small claim or a fast-track case without a solicitor.

They will also help if you are being taken to court by someone else because of one of these issues - or have already been without your knowledge.

You might take a case about poor service (if your plumbing leaked or your building work was never finished), unpaid invoices, a broken contract, negligence or if you were injured and it was not your fault.

You  would usually take someone to court so that you get a County Court Judgment (CCJ) that says they have to pay you money so that you don't lose out because of a problem that they caused.

####

Small claim =  under £10,000

Fast track claim = £10,000 - £25,000
